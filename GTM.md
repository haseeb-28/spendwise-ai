# GTM — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific person is:

**Engineering Manager or CTO at a seed-to-Series-A SaaS company (5–40 people), who recently noticed their AWS or SaaS bill growing faster than their team.**

More specifically: someone who just got a Slack message from their CEO asking "why did our tool costs jump?" and now has 48 hours to explain it. They have budget authority, they're technical enough to understand the recommendations, and they feel personal embarrassment about not having caught this sooner.

Secondary: the **solo founder or indie hacker** running a product with 2–5 AI API integrations, paying $300–800/month across services, who has never compared their spend against alternatives because there's no obvious place to do that comparison.

- "cursor vs github copilot 2025 pricing"

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Paid Budget)

**Week 1 — Seeding via content:**

Post a detailed Twitter/X thread: "We audited AI tool spend for 20 startups. Here's what we found." Real numbers, specific findings (anonymized). End with the tool link. Target: 5–10 early users from retweets.

**Week 2 — Hacker News launch:**

Submit as "Show HN: SpendWise — free AI tool spend auditor for startups." Timing: Tuesday 9am EST. The combination of "free," "no login," and "instant results" is HN-friendly. Target: 30–50 users from a midrange HN post.

**Week 3 — Cold outreach to founder communities:**

DM 50 founders on Indie Hackers and Twitter who have publicly complained about AI costs. Not a pitch — share the tool and ask for feedback. Attach a screenshot of a sample audit. Target: 20 additional users + 3–5 shared publicly.

**Week 4 — Content distribution:**

Submit a guest post to Lenny's Newsletter community (not the main newsletter — the Slack/forum). Topic: "How we reduced our AI tool spend by 32%." Real case study from a beta user. Target: 20–30 additional sign-ups.

Total realistic: 80–120 users in 30 days from these four channels.

---

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex's existing customer base.

Credex already has relationships with companies spending on AI infrastructure. A simple email to existing Credex customers ("We built a free tool to audit your AI spend — here's your personalized link") converts at 40–60% open rate because there's existing trust. No other audit tool has this warm list.

Second unfair channel: the audit results page itself is designed to be shared. When someone finds $400/month in savings, they forward the link to their co-founder or CFO. The OG preview shows the savings number prominently. This is a built-in referral loop that compounds with every user who saves money.

## Week-1 Traction If This Works

If the HN post lands well:

- 200–400 unique visitors on launch day
- 80–150 audits completed (40% completion rate on landing → form)
- 25–40 email captures (25–30% of completed audits)
- 3–5 high-savings leads flagged for Credex follow-up
- 2–3 people tweet/post about their savings → second-order traffic

Success signal: If 5+ people share their audit result URL publicly within 48 hours of launch, the viral loop is working.
